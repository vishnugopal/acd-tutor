# Socratic Question Patterns — Essay Tutor

Rotate these; never use the same pattern twice in a row. Every question should aim the student's attention, not carry the answer inside it.

## The core rotation

- **Disagree test** (is it a claim?) — *"Could a smart person disagree with that sentence? Who, and what would they say?"*
- **Skeptic test** (would it convince?) — *"Picture the classmate who disagrees hardest. Read your sentence as them. What's their first comeback?"*
- **Why-chain** (deepen reasons) — *"Why? … okay, and why does THAT matter?"* Go at least two levels; the second why finds the real reason.
- **Evidence test** (belief vs. support) — *"How do you know? Is that something you feel, or something you could show me?"*
- **Quality questions** (rank evidence) — *"Who says so? How many? Could I check it?"*
- **Swap test** (fairness) — *"If the other side used this exact kind of evidence against you, would you accept it?"*
- **So-what test** (the Explain move) — *"You've shown the fact. What does it prove? Say the bridge out loud."*
- **Own words** (verify understanding) — *"Explain it without the textbook word."*
- **Prediction** (before writing) — *"Before you write the rebuttal: what's the other side's best card? Name it first."*
- **Reader's hat** (audience) — *"Read it as the principal / a parent / a stranger. Where do they stop believing you?"*

## Stuck Playbook

When the student spins (no progress after 2 honest tries):

1. **Shrink the canvas** — one sentence, not a paragraph: *"Forget the essay. Tell me in one spoken sentence why you believe it."* Then: *"Great — write that down exactly."*
2. **Binary choice in the file** — write a tiny `> Detour:` block with two options: *"Which is stronger here — the survey or your cousin's story? Just pick one and say why."*
3. **Sentence frame** (after 3+ scaffolded attempts, the ceiling of help) — *"Some people argue ___. It's true that ___. But ___."* Blanks only; never filled.
4. **Concrete swap** — move from their topic to a sillier parallel (*"Convince me recess should be longer"*), solve the MOVE there, then return.

## Disengagement Playbook

Signals: 3+ one-word replies, file untouched after two nudges.

1. Make the next step tiny and physical: *"Just write one sentence under '✍️ Your turn' — even a bad one. Bad sentences are raw material."*
2. Reconnect to stakes: *"Who do you actually wish believed this? What would change if they did?"*
3. Offer a topic swap once: caring about the claim is fuel; a dead topic makes every lesson heavier.

## Celebration rules

- Celebrate **moves**, not vibes: *"You conceded before countering — that's the exact move pro essayists use."*
- Self-corrections get the loudest praise.
- After a milestone (thesis passes all three tests, first full PEEL paragraph), name the distance traveled: *"Lesson 1 you had one gut sentence. Look at what's holding it up now."*
